# Connect a 360 Photo Booth to ChackTok

This guide covers the standard Bluetooth connection workflow for supported 360 Photo Booth devices.

## When to Use This Guide

- You need to connect a supported 360 Photo Booth to ChackTok.
- You need to test rotation control before an event.
- Bluetooth connection is unstable or failing.

## What to Prepare

- Phone or tablet with ChackTok installed.
- Supported 360 Photo Booth with power on.
- Bluetooth enabled on the phone/tablet.
- Required system permissions enabled for ChackTok.
- Enough physical distance clearance around the booth for safe testing.

## Connection Steps

1. Power on the 360 Photo Booth and confirm the controller is ready.
2. Enable Bluetooth on the phone or tablet.
3. Open ChackTok.
4. Enter the device connection area.
5. Select the booth from the device list.
6. Wait for the connection status to confirm.
7. Open the controller or event setup page.
8. Test start, stop, direction, speed, and duration controls where available.
9. Run a short test capture before guests arrive.

## Basic Control Check

Before the event starts, verify:

- The booth starts and stops correctly.
- Rotation direction matches the event setup.
- Speed is appropriate for the camera and guest safety.
- Emergency stop or safe stop behavior is understood by the operator.
- The phone/tablet remains close enough for a stable connection.

## Common Connection Problems

| Problem | Likely cause | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Booth does not appear | Bluetooth or booth power not ready | Restart Bluetooth and power-cycle the booth |
| Connection drops | Distance or interference | Move closer and reduce nearby Bluetooth interference |
| Booth already connected | Another phone/tablet is paired | Disconnect the other device |
| Controls do not respond | Model/firmware mismatch or controller issue | Confirm compatibility and retest controller |
| App permissions missing | System permission disabled | Enable Bluetooth/location permission where required |

## Event Safety Notes

- Do not test rotation while guests are standing too close.
- Keep cables, bags, and props outside the booth path.
- Assign one operator to monitor the booth during live operation.
- Stop testing if rotation behavior is inconsistent.
