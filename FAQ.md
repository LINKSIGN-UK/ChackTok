Question1:What is Chacktok?
Answer:
Chacktok is a mobile application designed specifically for managing and operating 360 and iPad photobooths. It provides a comprehensive set of tools for event photography and videography, making it ideal for occasions like weddings, birthdays, corporate events, and parties. Key features include:

Wireless Control: Control various photobooth models directly from the app, including starting/stopping rotation for 360 booths.
Event Management: Create, customize, and manage events with easy-to-use templates.
Multi-device Support: Connect multiple devices (digital cameras, GoPros, TVs for display) and printers for instant photo printing.
Advanced Editing Options: Apply special effects, filters, frames, and custom overlays.
Efficient Sharing: Quickly share videos via SMS, QR codes, or social media.
Chacktok is widely used for its stability and broad compatibility with various photobooth hardware brands, making it a popular choice for event planners and photographers.

Question 12:GoPro file transfer too slow?
Answer:
If GoPro file transfer is slow in Chacktok, here are a few things to check to improve the transfer speed:
Adjust Video Resolution and Frame Rate:
GoPro files can be large, especially at high resolutions and frame rates. To speed up transfer, reduce the resolution and frame rate in your GoPro’s settings to create smaller files that transfer more quickly.
Check Network Quality:
For wireless transfers, a strong and stable Wi-Fi connection is essential. If your network is slow or unstable, consider switching to a different Wi-Fi network or using a wired connection.
Optimize Wi-Fi Usage:
If other devices are using the same Wi-Fi network, this can slow down the transfer. Disconnect unnecessary devices, or use a separate network to improve speed.
Consider a Wired Connection:
If wireless transfer continues to be slow, try connecting your GoPro to your mobile device with a USB cable for faster, more stable transfers.
These steps should help enhance the speed of your GoPro file transfers in Chacktok.

Question 13:GoPro freezes/crashes/reports errors when using it
Answer:
If your GoPro freezes, crashes, or reports errors when using it with Chacktok, try these troubleshooting steps:
Restart the GoPro:
Power off your GoPro, wait a few seconds, and turn it back on. Restarting can clear temporary issues that might cause freezing or crashing.
Update Firmware:
Ensure that your GoPro has the latest firmware version, as updates often include bug fixes that improve stability. You can check for updates in the GoPro app.
Reduce Video Settings:
High-resolution settings or high frame rates may strain your GoPro, especially during extended use. Lower the resolution and frame rate to improve performance.
Check Battery and Storage:
Low battery levels or a nearly full memory card can lead to crashes. Ensure the GoPro is fully charged and has sufficient storage space.
Use a Wired Connection:
If using a wireless connection, try switching to a USB cable connection, as it’s more stable and may prevent crashes.
Contact Support if Needed:
If the issue persists, you may need to reach out to GoPro support or Chacktok support at support@chacktok.com for further assistance.
